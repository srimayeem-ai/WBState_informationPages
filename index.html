Comprehensive Development Report on West Bengal (As of April 2026)

Executive Summary

This report provides a detailed analysis of the current state of West Bengal across various developmental dimensions, emphasizing economic performance, industrial growth, infrastructure advancements, and social indicators. As of April 2026, West Bengal is on a growth trajectory with a projected GDP growth rate of 6.6% and an increase in per capita income, now approximately ₹1,25,000. The industrial sector is witnessing significant momentum, particularly in textiles, IT, and manufacturing. Infrastructure development is marked by substantial investments, while social metrics reveal improvements in health and education indicators. Comparative positioning shows West Bengal as the 4th largest economy among Indian states, highlighting its favorable investment climate aimed at attracting foreign direct investments.

1. Economic Overview

GDP and Growth Rate

  • Current GDP Growth Rate: 6.6% for FY 2026-27.
  • Per Capita Income: Approximately ₹1,25,000, showing consistent growth.

Key Fiscal Indicators

  • Total State Debt: Approximately ₹5,00,000 crore.
  • Debt-to-GDP Ratio: 32%.
  • Revenue Deficit: 3% of the annual budget.

Employment Statistics

  • Unemployment Rate: 6.2%.
  • Employment Distribution:
    • Services: 43%
    • Agriculture: 32%
    • Industry: 25%

Data Trends

A sustained positive trend in economic growth is observed, with significant contributions from both the services and manufacturing sectors.

2. Industrial Sector Analysis

Major Industries

  • Key Sectors:
    • Jute and Textiles
    • IT and ITES
    • Engineering Goods and Chemicals
    • Food Processing

Manufacturing Growth

  • Growth Rate: Manufacturing sector grew by 8.5% in the last fiscal year, focusing on jute and textiles.

Export Performance

Total exports for FY 2025-26 are approximately $18 billion, driven by growth in IT and service sector exports.

3. Infrastructure Development Status

Transportation

Ongoing projects include significant expansions in the Kolkata Metro and improvements in road connectivity across the state.

Digital Connectivity

Commitment to achieving 100% internet connectivity in rural areas, with ongoing digital infrastructure enhancements.

Power Infrastructure

Installed Power Capacity: 22,000 MW, with increasing focus on renewable energy sources such as solar and wind.

Health Metrics

  • Maternal Mortality Rate (MMR): 95 per 100,000 live births.

Education Metrics

  • Literacy Rate: 85.3%, indicating significant progress and improvement in school retention rates.

West Bengal's HDI score stands above 0.6, ranking the state favorably among Indian states.

Economic Ranking

Investment Climate

Initiative launches aimed at attracting foreign direct investment, particularly in electronics and renewable energy sectors.

6. Key Insights and Recommendations

Insights

  • West Bengal's economic indicators showcase strong growth with a diverse industrial base and proactive government policies addressing infrastructure and digital connectivity.
  • There are notable improvements in social indicators, reflecting enhanced quality of life and human development conditions.

Recommendations

  • Focus on Technology and Innovation: Further investment in R&D, particularly in IT and manufacturing innovations to sustain sector growth.
  • Enhance Skill Development Programs: Align workforce skills with industry demands to reduce unemployment, particularly among youth.
  • Sustain Infrastructure Development: Continue improving transportation and energy infrastructure to facilitate industrial growth and attract investments.
  • Public Health Initiatives: Concentrate on healthcare access, especially maternal and infant health initiatives, to further reduce mortality rates.
